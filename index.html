Course Paper: Virtual Investment for Real Gains

This study aims to analyze how the digital stock market simulators will influence the financial literacy and education of students at the university level. Relevant studies assess the usefulness of using digital simulators, the actual impacts for students are various, including the cognitive, behavioral, and affective learning dimensions. Understanding these various impacts is essential for educators in the business area to design curricula effectively and efficiently for students.

Take Aways

● Within stock market simulators, individuals can take on the role of investors, where they can invest in different stocks and then build on their investment portfolios.
● The goal of finance literacy specializing in stock market trading is about modern portfolio theory, fundamental and technical analysis, behavioral finance, and financial risk management.
● Relevant studies assess the usefulness of using digital simulators, the actual impacts for students are various, including the cognitive, behavioral, and affective learning dimensions.
● Three themes are generated about the impact of stock market simulators on financial literacy for students in higher education, which are the application of financial literacy to practice, changes in risk tolerance, and financial decision-making skills.
● Instructional designers specializing in financial literacy are responsible for promoting more active and flexible learning experiences for students within higher education.

Online Assignment: Learning Not to Take the Bait

The online Assignment is about identifying, reading, and summarizing a scientific article, which is Learning Not to Take the Bait: A Longitudinal Examination of Digital Training Methods and Overlearning on Phishing Susceptibility. Based on the previous study that supports digital training as a way to reduce phishing susceptibility, this study examines the effectiveness and retention of two different digital training methods for phishing awareness over a longer period, and whether overlearning can improve these outcomes. Results show that compared to rule-based training, mindfulness training resulted in greater retention in terms of better email discrimination and less susceptibility to phishing attacks but similar levels of caution towards phishing. However, overlearning provided limited support for phishing skill retention.

Take Aways

● This study provides training sessions of rule-based training, mindfulness training, and control group training for participant students. All the training groups included some participants who received the overlearning procedure.
● The evaluation is conducted through email identification tests and mock phishing messages delivered to their inboxes over both the short term and long term.
● This article involves three groups with different time periods for comparison, and an additional factor of whether overlearning is implemented, resulting in seven hypotheses.
● Different age groups, such as teenagers, middle-aged individuals, or the elderly, might have varying susceptibilities to scams. Many further studies could be considered or discussed.
● In this AI-driven era where phishing activities become increasingly complex and even pass through the security filter, the personal level of anti-phishing is really helpful.

Group Work: Constructivism Theory

Group work is about summarizing the perspective in terms of its theoretical conceptualizations for constructivism. Constructivism is about mental activity, the environment, and experience. It focuses on the interaction between the environment and mind, where humans create meaning through cumulative elaboration and reflection. Learning occurs by creating meaning from experiences, and memory is constructed through a history of interactions. The flexible use of pre-existing knowledge is preferred over rote recall.

Take Aways

● Learning is shaped by personal experiences, requiring an examination of the learner’s experiences. Transfer of knowledge occurs through authentic tasks and reflection.
● Emphasizes active engagement, problem-solving skills, and assessment focused on knowledge transfer. Collaboration and reflection are encouraged.
● Focuses on collaboration, multiple perspectives, and self-chosen learning paths. Flexible learning objectives promote deeper engagement and critical thinking.
● Constructivist assessments focus on peer review, virtual exhibitions, and critical discussions.
● Constructivist approaches can struggle with minimally guided instructions, lack of tangible objects, and a one-size-fits-all curriculum.
